# How fast should a local service business respond to a new online lead inquiry?

**Short answer:** A local service business should respond to a new online lead inquiry in under one minute. If the owner, dispatcher, or front desk cannot reply that fast every time, the response layer should be automated with instant SMS, an AI receptionist, or both.

For plumbers, HVAC companies, dentists, roofers, med spas, and law firms, the lead is usually active right now. They have a problem, they have their phone in their hand, and they are comparing multiple providers. The fastest credible response often becomes the booked appointment.

## Why the one-minute window matters

Local service buyers do not wait like enterprise software buyers. A homeowner with a leak, a patient with tooth pain, or a business owner trying to schedule a repair will usually contact more than one provider. Once one provider answers, asks the right questions, and confirms the next step, the search often ends.

That is why speed-to-lead is not just a sales metric. It is an operational requirement. A slow callback can make a strong local business look unavailable even when the team is excellent.

## What should happen after a form fill

The first response should do three things:

- Confirm that the request was received.
- Ask one useful qualifying question.
- Move the lead toward a booked call, appointment, quote, or emergency dispatch.

A simple "we will get back to you soon" message is not enough. The answer should feel like the beginning of service, not a receipt.

For example, a form submission from a homeowner asking about AC repair can trigger an immediate SMS asking whether the system is fully down, whether there are unusual sounds or leaks, and whether the customer needs same-day service. That keeps the lead engaged while the business decides whether a human should take over.

## What should happen after a missed call

Missed calls need the same urgency. If a call is not answered, the business should send a text-back within seconds, not hours. The message should acknowledge the missed call and ask what the caller needs help with.

For many local service businesses, missed-call recovery is the highest-leverage fix because the caller already showed intent. The business does not need more traffic first. It needs to stop leaking the calls it already earns.

An [AI receptionist](/features/ai-receptionist/) can answer the call directly, qualify the job, and book the next step. When a human call is still needed, the AI or SMS flow can collect the details so the callback is specific and useful.

## When automation is the right answer

Automation is the right answer when the business cannot guarantee fast human coverage during peak hours, after hours, weekends, lunch breaks, or job-site work. That describes most local service businesses.

The goal is not to replace every human conversation. The goal is to make sure every lead receives an immediate first response, every urgent request is identified, and every qualified prospect is moved toward a booking.

## FAQ

### What is the best response time for a local service lead?

Under one minute is the practical target. The lead is still active, the problem is still top of mind, and the customer has not yet settled on another provider.

### Is five minutes fast enough for local service leads?

Five minutes is better than an hour, but it is not the safest target. In urgent local-service categories, a customer can call and book another business in less than five minutes.

### What if my team cannot answer every inquiry that fast?

Use automation for the first response. Instant SMS, missed-call text-back, and an AI receptionist can acknowledge the lead, ask qualifying questions, and route or book the next step before a human is available.
